Reduce Post Harvest Loss

Post-harvest loss means the loss of crops, fruits, and vegetables after harvesting due to poor storage, packaging, transportation, or handling methods. It affects farmers' income and food availability. To reduce post-harvest loss, farmers need to follow proper techniques and services.

Key Services to Reduce Post Harvest Loss:

1. Proper Storage

Use modern storage systems like cold storage, moisture-proof containers, and climate-controlled warehouses to increase the shelf life of crops, fruits, and vegetables.

2. Better Packaging

Use eco-friendly and protective packaging materials to avoid damage during transportation and handling of crops and fruits.

3. Timely Transportation

Transport crops and vegetables quickly using proper vehicles and methods to avoid spoilage due to delay.

4. Training Farmers

Provide training to farmers about modern harvesting, grading, storage, and handling methods to avoid post-harvest loss.

5. Benefits of Reducing Post Harvest Loss

Increase farmers' income, reduce wastage of food, improve food quality, and meet market demand efficiently.
