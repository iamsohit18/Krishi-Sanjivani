🌾Disc Plough

A Disc Plough is a primary tillage implement used in agriculture for deep soil plowing. It is ideal for cutting, lifting, turning, and mixing soil. It is especially effective in hard, dry, stony, or sticky soils where traditional ploughs may not perform well.

🔧 Main Components of a Disc Plough

  • Disc Blades – Concave steel discs that cut and turn the soil.
  • Disc Bearings & Spindles – Allow smooth rotation of discs.
  • Frame – The main structure holding all parts together.
  • Scrapers – Clean soil stuck on discs.
  • Hitch/Three-Point Linkage – Attaches the plough to a tractor.
  • Stand/Leg Support – Helps in parking when not in use.
  • Depth Adjustment Mechanism – Adjusts the working depth of the plough.

⚙️ Working Mechanism of a Disc Plough

  • The plough is attached to a tractor via a three-point linkage.
  • As the tractor moves, the rotating discs cut into the soil.
  • The concave shape lifts and turns the soil sideways.
  • The scrapers ensure soil doesn’t stick to the discs.
  • This process buries weeds, residues, and loosens the soil for sowing.

🌱 Primary Uses

  • Initial soil breaking
  • Turning soil to bury crop residues and weeds
  • Mixing fertilizers or compost
  • Preparing hard, virgin, or rocky land for cultivation

✅ Advantages of Using a Disc Plough

  • Works well in hard, dry, and rocky soils
  • Bury crop residues effectively
  • Prevents soil erosion on slopes
  • Increases aeration and moisture retention
  • Can handle stony and root-filled fields
  • Durable and requires less maintenance

🧰 Maintenance Tips

  • Regularly inspect and tighten all bolts and fasteners
  • Sharpen or replace disc blades when worn
  • Grease bearings and moving parts routinely
  • Clean mud and crop residue after use
  • Store in a dry, covered place
  • Inspect scrapers and adjust if needed

⚠️ Safety Precautions

  • Only trained operators should use the disc plough
  • Always turn off the tractor before maintenance
  • Keep safe distance from rotating discs
  • Use appropriate PPE (gloves, boots, etc.)
  • Avoid using in overly wet or steep fields
  • Regularly check for cracks or damage in the frame and discs

📋 Requirements Before Use

  • Check disc sharpness and alignment
  • Adjust working depth based on soil type
  • Verify tractor compatibility (HP requirement)
  • Lubricate all moving parts
  • Clear field of large stones or debris
  • Conduct a trial run to calibrate settings

🌾 Suitable Crops for Use

  • Paddy (for land leveling and puddling)
  • Wheat and Barley
  • Sugarcane
  • Maize and Corn
  • Pulses and Chickpeas
  • Cotton
  • Groundnut
  • Vegetables like Potato and Onion
